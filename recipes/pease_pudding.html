Pease Pudding

Description

Pease pudding is one of those rare foods outside of north-east England, which is unfortunate if you're like me and used to it growing up, now you've moved away and can't find it anywhere. So you decide to look up a recipe to make it only to find there's no real recipes for it, adding unecessary ingredients and such.

You may be happy to know that it's as simple as shove it in the pan and leave it. Which is a great thing, and it doesn't need many ingredients at all; take a look.

Ingredients

  • Yellow split peas
  • Whole onion, usually brown.
  • Water

Yes, that's really all there is to it. Now onto the method.

Steps

  1. Set 750ml of water to boil in a pan.
  2. Add 250g of yellow split peas.
  3. Cut the top and botton from the onion, peel it, and add it to the water whole.
  4. Set to a medium heat, on an induction I would go with 4.
